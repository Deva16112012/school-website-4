What's new

  • International Day of Persons with Disabilities

    The observance of the Day aims to promote an understanding of disability issues and mobilize support for the dignity, rights and well-being of persons with disabilities. It also seeks to increase awareness of gains to be derived from the integration of persons with disabilities in every aspect of political, social, economic and cultural life. To celebrate togetherness, the students of Vydehi School of Excellence were part of the classroom activities in Vydehi School, a center for children with developmental difficulties. The apprehensions were seen initially, which changed to amazement as they realized what the children can do with limitations in some areas. This feeling was echoed by Zia, “I felt very happy to work with them. While coming I was little tense but it was fun with Priyanshi and Arjun”. The students of Vydehi School of Excellence showed a great deal of enthusiasm in organizing music, dance, art and magic show performances for the students. It was a day filled with learning experiences for students studying in both the schools.

  • Autism Awareness Month

    Vydehi Institute of Rehabilitation marked its celebrations of ‘Autism Awareness Month’ by a lot fun filled - therapeutic Activities.   • Autism Awareness Initiative

    April is Autism awareness month and Vydehi School initiated awareness– raising event by taking a ride on the Metro rail. The students carried placards, slogans and information on early diagnosis of autism and early intervention for autism. They were accompanied by staff, parents and other supporters / volunteers. The students found support in the staff members of Namma Metro, who were instrumental in making the ride safe and pleasurable. Brief History: On December 18, 2007, the United Nations General Assembly adopted resolution which declares April 2 as World Autism Awareness Day. The World Autism Awareness Day resolution encourages all to take measures to raise awareness about autism throughout society and to encourage early diagnosis and early intervention. It further expresses deep concern at the prevalence and high rate of autism in children in all regions of the world and the consequent developmental challenges.
